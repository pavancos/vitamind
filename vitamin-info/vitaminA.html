Vitamin A:

Vitamin A is a fat-soluble vitamin. Its different forms are often called "retinoids." Vitamin A is needed for the proper growth and functioning of many parts of the body, including the eyes, skin, and immune system.
People most commonly use vitamin A for treating aging skin, acne, HIV/AIDS, cataracts, child development, infections, and many other conditions.

Food Sources :

  • Animal Sources:
    • Liver and other organ meats (such as beef liver, chicken liver)
    • Fish oil
    • Eggs (especially the yolk)
  • Dairy Products:
    • Milk
    • Cheese
    • Butter
  • Fruits:
    • Mangoes
    • Watermelon
  • Vegetables:
    • Carrots
    • Sweet potatoes
    • Spinach
    • Broccoli
